# Cover Letter Template & Guide
# Used by Agent 5 (Cover Letter Writer)

---

## CORE PRINCIPLES

1. **Never summarize the resume** — the letter adds context the resume can't
2. **One story, one hook** — lead with something specific about THIS company
3. **The veteran angle is a superpower** — use it, don't hide it
4. **Under 400 words** — hiring managers read for 30 seconds
5. **Match the JD's tone** — startup = casual/energetic; enterprise = professional/precise
6. **End with action** — ask for the interview

---

## STRUCTURE (4 paragraphs)

### Paragraph 1 — The Hook (2–3 sentences)
**Goal:** Make them keep reading. Show you know them.

Formula: [Specific thing about company/team/product] + [why it connects to you personally]

Examples:
- "Your team's work on [specific product/tech] caught my attention because [genuine reason]. As a CS senior at UT Dallas finishing a [related project], I've been following [company] closely."
- "Most people don't expect a Marine Corps veteran to be building ML pipelines — but that's exactly what I've been doing at UT Dallas, and [Company]'s approach to [specific thing] is exactly where I want to apply that work."

**Do NOT start with:** "I am writing to apply for..." / "I am excited to apply..."

---

### Paragraph 2 — Your Proof (3–4 sentences)
**Goal:** Connect your #1 most relevant experience to their #1 most important need.

Pull from: master_resume.md + JD requirements
Formula: [Specific project/achievement] + [quantified result] + [connection to their need]

Example:
- "For my senior capstone, I led a team of 6 developers building a real estate price prediction system for Dallas County using XGBoost, PostGIS, and FastAPI. We processed [X] property records and achieved [X]% model accuracy — practical experience with exactly the kind of production ML pipelines [Company] is building. Before that, four years as a Marine Corps Military Police Officer gave me something most new grads lack: the ability to execute under pressure, lead teams through ambiguity, and own outcomes completely."

---

### Paragraph 3 — Why Them (2–3 sentences)
**Goal:** Prove this isn't a form letter. Show you've done research.

Research hooks to use:
- Recent product launch / blog post / GitHub repo
- Engineering blog post or tech talk by their team
- Company mission that aligns with your values
- Specific team or problem space you're interested in
- Veteran hiring programs (if applicable: Goldman VIP, Microsoft MSSA, etc.)

Example:
- "I'm specifically drawn to [Company]'s work on [specific thing] — I read [engineer name]'s post on [topic] and it lines up directly with the ML work I've been doing. The fact that [Company] [specific cultural thing] tells me this is an environment where I'd both contribute immediately and grow quickly."

---

### Paragraph 4 — The Close (2 sentences)
**Goal:** Ask for the meeting. Make it easy to say yes.

Formula: [Confident summary] + [clear ask]

Example:
- "I'd welcome the chance to walk through my projects and talk about how my background maps to what your team is building. I'm available for a conversation at your convenience — [email] or [phone]."

**Do NOT end with:** "Thank you for your time and consideration." (weak)
**Do end with:** A direct ask.

## TONE GUIDE BY COMPANY TYPE

| Company Type | Tone | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Early-stage startup | Energetic, direct, slightly casual | Stiff corporate language |
| Growth-stage tech | Professional but conversational | Overly formal or stuffy |
| FAANG / large tech | Clear, confident, technical credibility | Hype, fluff, vague claims |
| Finance/Goldman | Professional, precise, buttoned-up | Anything casual or breezy |
| Defense/Gov contractor | Formal, emphasize clearance potential | Casual or opinionated |
| Consulting | Business value, impact metrics | Pure tech jargon |

## VETERAN FRAMING GUIDE

**Use the military angle when:**
- The role values leadership, ownership, or accountability
- The company has a veteran hiring program
- The JD mentions "high-stakes", "mission-critical", "24/7", "incident response"
- You're competing with candidates who have more CS internships

**Frame it as:**
- "The Marine Corps taught me to deliver results when it matters most — not just in ideal conditions."
- "I've led people in situations where failure wasn't an option. That wiring doesn't go away when I'm debugging a production incident at 2am."
- "Four years of making high-stakes decisions with incomplete information translates directly to [relevant engineering skill]."

**Do NOT frame it as:**
- Seeking sympathy or veteran preference
- A reason you're behind other candidates
- Something separate from your CS skills (it's additive, not alternative)
